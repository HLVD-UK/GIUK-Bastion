◆ GIUK BASTION
NORTH ATLANTIC · SEABED WARFARE · TURN-BASED SIMULATION

The situation. Tension with Russia is short of war but sharpening. The Northern Fleet has dispersed and three submarines are unaccounted for, moving to hold Euro-Atlantic energy pipelines and undersea data cables at risk — the seabed being the one front line nobody can see.

Your command. A Royal Navy hybrid force: the Type 26 frigate HMS Glasgow, the attack submarine HMS Astute, a P-8 Poseidon maritime patrol aircraft, the seabed-operations ship RFA Proteus, and the uncrewed CETUS XLUUV. Five units to cover an ocean.

Your mission. Hold the GIUK gap for 16 turns. Find and prosecute the submarines before they reach the cables and pipelines. Shadow the grey-zone vessels loitering in the Channel — you cannot engage them, only deter. Keep the lights on and the data flowing.

Each turn: issue movement orders, then END TURN. The enemy acts, and an intelligence brief follows — OSINT, media, and allied reporting that hints at what's coming without giving it away. Weigh it carefully. Not all of it is reliable.
